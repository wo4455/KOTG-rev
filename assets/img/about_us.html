about us

Kids on the Grow offers fun, creative and multi-sensory children's classes with a focus on small class size. Each program was developed by experts in the field of child development to encourage curiosity, build self- confidence and foster motor and language skills.

Our Owners

Elizabeth Urso | PT, DPT, PCS

Elizabeth has been a practicing pediatric physical therapist for 15 years in the Chicago region after graduating from the University of Illinois at Chicago. Elizabeth has worked in both hospital-based and outpatient environments, including The Rehabilitation Institute of Chicago and Pathways Center for Children. She has focused on pediatric care in the Illinois Early Intervention Program since forming her own practice in 2003. As her practice grew, she obtained her Doctorate in Physical Therapy from Boston University. Elizabeth completed her board certification as a pediatric clinical specialist in 2007, is a credentialed Illinois Early Intervention Specialist and Evaluator, and is an active member in the American Physical Therapy Association. Elizabeth's dedication to her patients is the driving force behind her success. As a mother of a young family, her passion for children and their development makes even the youngest patient feel comfortable.

Claudette Bravo | MA, CCC-SLP

Claudette graduated from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign with a Master's degree in Speech-Language Pathology. She is currently enrolled in a Ph.D program at the University of Illinois at Chicago within the College of Education through the Reading, Writing, & Literacy program. Claudette has specialized in pediatrics for the past 15 years with clinical experiences in school-based settings and private practice. Claudette is a mother of two young children who has transformed her love of children and interest in research into creative, successful treatment interventions for children with speech and language disorders. Claudette is a certified member of ISHA (Illinois Speech-Hearing Association) and ASHA (American Speech-Language Hearing Association). She is also a credentialed Early Intervention Specialist and Evaluator through the Illinois Early Intervention Program.

Our Therapists

Katie Henry | PT, DPT

Katie graduated from Duke University with her Doctorate of Physical Therapy after receiving her BS in Biology and Psychology from Loyola University of Chicago. Katie has always had a passion for working with children and has been practicing in pediatric physical therapy for 5 years. Her areas of interest include cerebral palsy, brain injury, aquatics, vestibular rehab, group therapy, early mobility for children with disabilities (Go Baby Go program), physical fitness & wellness for children with disabilities and community reintegration. Katie has provided mentoring for therapists in pediatrics and has advanced competencies in aquatic physical therapy, gait training and serial casting. Katie has worked in inpatient and outpatient pediatric rehabilitation at The Rehabilitation Institute of Chicago (RIC), in home private practice PT serving children birth to three years, aquatic PT for children of all ages, research physical therapist for patients with mild traumatic brain injury and vestibular deficits and played a major role in developing the pediatric program at RIC. Katie is a credentialed Early Intervention Specialist, certified pediatric vestibular specialist, and certified kids yoga instructor. Katie is responsible for implementing program development at Kids on the Grow. She is very passionate about working with children and is excited to share her energy, creativity, passion and unique abilities with the children and families in our practice.

Occupational Therapists

Courtney Smith | MS, OTR/L

Courtney earned her Master’s degree in Occupational Therapy from Colorado State University. Her passion for helping children reach their full potential began as a young adult. During her graduate studies, she was published in the American Journal of Occupational Therapy for her shared graduate research in neurological markers for sensory processing disorder (SPD). She is a provider of the S.O.S. (Sequential Oral Sensory) approach to feeding for children with mild to severe feeding difficulties. Courtney has experience working with children with SPD, Down syndrome, Autism, feeding difficulties, visual and hearing impairments, and various neurological disorders. She provides therapy both in the home and in schools and feels incredibly blessed to be a part of each child and family’s journey. She is a credentialed Illinois Early Intervention Specialist and Evaluator.

Vanessa Smith | M.S. Developmental Therapist

Vanessa is a Developmental Therapist who earned her Master’s degree in Child Development with a specialization in infant studies from Erikson Institute and completed her BA in Psychology at DePaul University. Vanessa has extensive experience working with children ranging from 2 – 12 years old with diagnoses of Autism Spectrum Disorder, Global Developmental Delay, ADHD and other neurodevelopmental disorders. Her professional experience includes providing individualized developmental and behavioral interventions in home and school settings utilizing multiple therapeutic approaches including ABA and DIR/Floortime. Vanessa is credentialed as an Early Intervention specialist providing developmental/ play therapy for children birth to 3 years who have special needs or are at-risk. She is trained in the administration of various assessment tools including the Bayley III, Vineland ECSE, and the Hawaii Early Learning Profile and has experience conducting diagnostic evaluations. Her work is strongly informed by Infant Mental Health approaches. She uses a family-centered, strengths-based approach in her individual therapy sessions.
